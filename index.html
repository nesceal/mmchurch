Malayang Mananampalataya

Ito ay isang rehistradong relihiyon na lumitaw sa Morong, Rizal, Pilipinas, magmula pa noong Agosto 10 taong 1995 - isang relihiyon ng mga nananalig sa Banal na Espiritu ng Diyos Anak na sumasapi sa katauhan ni Gng. Constantina S.A. San Diego (Bb. San Antonio noon)

Ang pangalang Malayang Mananampalataya ay napagkaisahan ng may 772 katao na siyang nagtatag ng relihiyong ito. Ang ibig sabihin ng salitang "malaya" sa M.M. ay ang bukas na pagtanggap ng mga kasapi anuman ang lipi o relihiyong kanilang kinabibilangan. Ngunit hindi nangangahulugang malayang makagagawa ng anumang bagay ang isang kasapi na labag sa mga alituntunin nito.

  • The Beginning

    In the Barrio of Maybancal, Municipality of Morong, Province of Rizal, lives Constantina Talanay San Antonio, the youngest and only daughter in a brood of four by the first marriage of Adriano Bautista San Antonio to Pacencia Talanay. Mr San Antonio worked as the Chief in the Department of Agriculture and Natural Resources. Pacencia died when Constantina was barely one year old so little Constantina was carefully reared by her father and her step mother, Marcelina Francisco.

  • At about 6 ½ years old she was sent to the Maybancal Barrio School for her primary education, then to the Tomas Claudio Memorial School where she obtained her intermédiate education. All the years of her childhood, Constantina showed signs of intelligence, kind-heartedness, truthfulness and above all very pious indeed.

  • From the Tomas Claudio Memorial School she enrolled at the Morong High School where one day when she was taking a monthly test in her 4th year class, her Pilipino teacher, Ms Virginia Aralar, found her leaning on her desk because she felt dizzy. Her kind teacher gave her first aid and a glass of water. When she regained her senses, Constantina recalled clearly that as she was trying to cope, her head tilted downward, someone in a long dress standing with bare feet caught her eyes and as she tried to look upward to see the face, she passed out. Also, she said somebody whispered to her that a letter was placed in her notebook and that she could read the letter. Constantina wasted no time opening her notebook and to her surprise she found the letter, which contained Godly advice. She was also touched to see that this letter was signed by her late real mother’s nickname,“Pace”. She gave the letter to her brother Francisco.

  • March 1953

    Tancing's Aura

    Constantina graduated from the Morong High School in March 1953. During the graduation exercises, Constantina played the role of a spirit of a dead mother in a script staged by the graduating class of that year and directed by Ms Liwayway A. Ramos. These script players were selected from a group of three who were given try –outs by faculty members of the Morong High School. During the play, Constantina showed an unusually fine behaviour on stage, her voice seemingly filled with holiness and many curious and keen observers said they noticed the aura of Constantina was brighter than the ordinary and was visible indeed.

  • 1955

    Lord's Will

    After graduation from high school in 1953, Constantina requested her parents that she be allowed to take Midwifery but as she was very young she had to wait for 1955 till she bécomes 18 years old, the accepted mínimum age in the Government School of Midwifery. When she took the exam in 1955, for one reason or another, she did not qualify. Later on, Constantina have known that it was our Lord’s will that she did not qualify because if she studied further, that may be the cause of her failure to attain her Godly destiny.

  • 1954

    A Letter

    One night at about 6 pm in 1954, Constantina ran in fright saying that she saw a big white hand placing a letter in the polo shirt pocket of her father which was then hanging against the wall of their house. The father was called to check the pocket and was surprised to see a letter requesting Mr San Antonio and his wife that Constantina’s wishes should be followed. This was quite unusual but the couple did not give much value to it.

  • April 1955

    White Cloth

    One night in April 1955, while Constantina was looking through the window she asked her father to go down the house to see and get a piece of white cloth about 1 meter square under a santol tree in their yard. When Mr San Antonio went to the place, he saw nothing so he lovingly exclaimed to his daughter, “Constantina, you are a good child, if you have nothing to do, i would suggest you please go to your bed.” Constantina reiterated she could see something but Mr. San Antonio was able to convince her to go to bed.

  • July 1955

    The Mysterious Man

    One day in July 1955, Constantina was washing clothes in the well about 10 meters away from their yard, feeling hungry she went up to the house alone. When she was eating she heard the call of whom she supposed was a beggar. She looked out and saw an old bearded man with a big cloth pouch tied on the cane he was holding. Constantina did not even had a cent to give as her step-mother went to the market. She was forced to ask for an excuse from the poor beggar. The beggar left followed by the eyes of Constantina and to her surprise, the beggar disappeared upon reaching the “is-is” tree just around five meters away from their house. When her step mother arrived, Constantina told the story to her, but again, she did not give much weight to the story.

  • August 10, 1955

    On August 10, 1955, Wednesday evening, at about 6 pm, Constantina again suffered a stroke of dizziness and stooped down on a table in their house. This table happened to be the table where the sto sepulcro of the Roman Catholic Church is Morong was laid every Good Friday for a string of years, as kins of her step mother once participated in the care of such sto sepulcro.
